Unifying Metrics Across a Fragmented BI Landscape

A Series C payments company had 15 different definitions of "revenue" across their tools. Every meeting started with "which number is right?" We built a single source of truth that restored confidence in their data.

The Client

A Series C payments infrastructure company processing over $2B in annual transaction volume. Their data team of 8 supported product, finance, operations, and executive stakeholders across a complex multi-product platform.

The Problem

After three years of rapid growth and multiple acquisitions, the company's analytics ecosystem had become a patchwork of conflicting definitions and siloed tools. The symptoms were unmistakable:

  • Finance reported monthly revenue of $8.2M while the product team showed $7.9M—and both had documentation proving their number was correct
  • Three different Looker dashboards, each with its own "revenue" calculation, were used by different executives
  • The dbt project had grown to 400+ models with minimal testing and documentation
  • Ad-hoc requests took 2-3 weeks because analysts spent more time reconciling numbers than answering questions

The VP of Analytics described the situation: "We'd reached a point where nobody trusted any number until we could trace it back to the source system. That's not sustainable."

Our Approach

Phase 1: Discovery & Assessment (Weeks 1-2)

We started with a comprehensive Analytics Health Assessment to understand the full scope of the problem. This involved:

  • Inventorying all metric definitions across Looker, Mode, and internal tools
  • Auditing the dbt project structure, test coverage, and documentation
  • Interviewing stakeholders to understand how they used data and where they lost trust
  • Mapping data lineage from source systems through to final dashboards

The assessment revealed the root cause: revenue calculations diverged at the staging layer due to inconsistent handling of refunds, chargebacks, and multi-currency transactions across different product lines.

Phase 2: Foundation Rebuild (Weeks 3-10)

Armed with clear diagnosis, we rebuilt the analytics foundation:

  • Unified staging layer: Standardized how all transaction types were ingested and categorized
  • Canonical metric definitions: Created a single, documented definition for each core metric with clear business logic
  • Restructured dbt project: Organized models into clear layers (staging, intermediate, marts) with consistent naming conventions
  • Comprehensive testing: Implemented tests for uniqueness, referential integrity, freshness, and business rules
  • Looker cleanup: Migrated saved queries to governed LookML with a single source of truth for each metric

Phase 3: Enablement & Transition (Weeks 11-12)

We ensured the team could maintain and extend what we built:

  • Conducted training sessions on the new architecture and best practices
  • Created comprehensive documentation for all metric definitions and business logic
  • Established code review processes and testing requirements for new development
  • Set up monitoring and alerting for data quality issues

The Outcome

Within the first month after go-live:

  • Metric discrepancy issues dropped by 73%
  • Ad-hoc request turnaround improved from 2-3 weeks to 2-3 days
  • The executive team began using a single revenue dashboard with confidence
  • Finance and Product aligned on numbers for the first time in over a year
"We went from spending 60% of our time answering questions about why numbers don't match to having stakeholders who actually trust our data. The ROI was clear within the first month."
— VP of Analytics

The company continues with a 20-hour monthly retainer for ongoing development and support as they expand into new product lines.
