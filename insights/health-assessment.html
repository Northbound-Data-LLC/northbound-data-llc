What to Expect from an Analytics Health Assessment

Thinking about getting an outside perspective on your analytics infrastructure? Here's exactly what happens during an assessment engagement—no surprises, no hidden agendas.

Why an Assessment?

Most organizations know something is wrong with their analytics infrastructure. What they often don't know is exactly what's wrong, how bad it is, or what to do about it.

That's where an assessment comes in. It's a structured diagnostic that gives you a clear, unbiased picture of your current state—and a roadmap for where to go. Think of it as a technical due diligence on your own data infrastructure.

We recommend assessments as the starting point for most engagements because good solutions require good diagnosis. Jumping straight to implementation without understanding the problem usually means solving the wrong thing.

What We Look At

An assessment examines your analytics ecosystem across several dimensions:

dbt Project Health

We review your dbt project structure, looking for:

  • Architecture and organization — Is there a clear layering strategy? Are models logically organized?
  • Model complexity — Are there overly complex models that should be broken up?
  • Performance — What are run times like? Where are the bottlenecks?
  • Documentation — Can someone new to the project understand what's happening?
  • Testing — What's the test coverage? Are the right things being tested?

We generate a health score that helps you benchmark against best practices and track improvement over time.

BI Query Ecosystem

Beyond the transformation layer, we inventory your BI tools:

  • What saved queries exist across Looker, Mode, Tableau, or other tools?
  • Which are actively used and which are orphaned?
  • Where is business logic implemented that should be in the transformation layer?
  • Are there duplicate or conflicting implementations of the same metrics?

Metric Consistency

We specifically look for metric discrepancies:

  • How are key metrics defined across different tools and teams?
  • Where do definitions diverge, and why?
  • What's the root cause of reported number mismatches?

Data Quality

We evaluate your data quality posture:

  • What testing exists? Is it comprehensive?
  • How are data quality issues detected and resolved?
  • What's the time-to-detection for common issues?
  • Do stakeholders trust the data? Why or why not?

The Process

Week 1: Discovery

We start by getting access and context. This involves:

  • Read-only access to your data warehouse and dbt project
  • Access to BI tools to inventory queries and dashboards
  • Kickoff meeting to understand your goals, pain points, and priorities
  • Stakeholder interviews with key data consumers (usually 4-6 conversations)

The goal is to understand not just the technical state, but the organizational context. What problems are actually causing pain? What's blocking progress?

Week 2: Analysis

This is where the deep work happens. We:

  • Audit the dbt project systematically
  • Inventory and analyze BI queries
  • Trace metric discrepancies to their source
  • Assess test coverage and data quality practices
  • Document findings and develop recommendations

We're looking for patterns, not just individual issues. Root causes matter more than symptoms.

Week 3: Synthesis & Delivery

We synthesize findings into actionable outputs:

  • Executive summary presentation (for leadership, non-technical)
  • Detailed technical findings report (for the data team)
  • Prioritized remediation roadmap with effort estimates
  • Final readout meeting to walk through findings and answer questions

What You Get

At the end of an assessment, you'll have:

Executive Summary

A presentation-ready overview suitable for leadership. It covers the high-level state, key risks, and strategic recommendations without getting into technical weeds.

Technical Findings Report

A detailed document covering everything we found, organized by area. Specific issues are documented with context and recommendations. This becomes a reference for your team as they address issues.

Prioritized Roadmap

A sequenced plan for remediation, organized into phases. Each phase has clear objectives, deliverables, and effort estimates. You'll know exactly what to do first, second, and third—and roughly what each phase will require.

Health Score

A quantified assessment of your dbt project's health across multiple dimensions. This gives you a baseline for measuring improvement and a way to track progress over time.

What Happens After

An assessment gives you clarity. What you do with that clarity is up to you. Common paths forward:

  • Internal remediation — Your team takes the roadmap and executes it themselves. The assessment provides the diagnosis; you provide the treatment.
  • Foundation engagement — If the issues are significant and you want expert help, we can move into an implementation engagement to address them.
  • Targeted sprints — If specific areas need attention (just metric alignment, just testing, etc.), we can scope focused sprints to address them.

There's no pressure to continue with us. Many clients take the assessment findings and run with them independently. Our goal is to give you the information you need to make good decisions—what you do with it is your call.

Is This Right for You?

An assessment makes sense if you:

  • Know something is wrong but aren't sure exactly what
  • Want an unbiased external perspective on your infrastructure
  • Need to build a business case for analytics investment
  • Are a new data leader inheriting an existing setup
  • Want a roadmap before committing to a larger engagement

It's less necessary if you already have a clear diagnosis and just need execution help. In that case, we can skip straight to implementation.
